How to play
The basics
  1. Spin & draft — Spin an era & team, then draft a 6-player squad: a flexible front five (3 Guards + 1 Forward + 1 Center, or 2 Guards + 2 Forwards + 1 Center) plus a Wildcard on the bench (anyone). Tap a player, then a spot, to move or swap.
  2. Win categories — Squads go head-to-head on 6 stats: Points, Rebounds, Assists, Steals, Blocks and Turnovers (fewer wins). Take the most categories to win the match — 1 point per category, ties break on total team eFG%.
  3. Clutch shooter — Once your squad is full, tap your buzzer-beater on the court (their shooting % stays hidden from opponents). If a match ends dead level, they take the last shot — scoring with a chance equal to their eFG%.
Three ways to play
🏆
Challenge Your FriendsA private online tournament. Create it, share the code, and everyone drafts. Once all players have joined and drafted it plays a round-robin — best record is champion. No-shows? Start with who’s in or drop them, then anyone kicks off the next round. Best for a small group, head-to-head over rounds.
🌎
Challenge the WorldOne global, all-time ladder. Submit a squad and it’s compared against everyone for a W–L record and a rank. Played in monthly seasons: whoever’s top when the month ends is crowned champion, then the board resets. Best for one squad vs. the whole world.
🏅
LeaguesYour own mini-world — public or private. Create or join one, then draft as many teams as you like (up to 10). They all compete on the league’s own all-time ladder. Best for an ongoing competition with a group you choose.
In short: Friends is a structured tournament with people you invite · the World is one giant monthly leaderboard · Leagues are your own ongoing mini-Worlds.
